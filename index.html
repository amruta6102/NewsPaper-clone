GUDI PADWA

Gudi Padwa 2024: Gudi Padwa is one of the main Hindu festival celebrated mainly in Maharashtra and Goa. This is celebrated by Maharashtrian and Konkani Hindus with immense fervor and enthusiasm. This festival has a great religious significance among Hindus. Gudi Padwa marks the beginning of Marathi New Year and also observed as the first as the first day of Chaitra Month. Gudi Padwa is also known as Samvatsar Padvo. This day is marks the beginning of Harvest season in Maharashtra. is one of the most important Hindu festival which is observed in Maharashtra and Goa by Marathi and Konkani Hindu people. This festival holds a great religious significance among them and they celebrate this festival with immense joy and happiness. This day is celebrated on the first day of Chaitra Month, which marks the starting of Marathi New Year. Gudi Padwa is a combination of two words Gudi means a flag or emblem and Padwa means the first day of Chaitra Shukla Paksha which is also known as Pratipada Tithi. History of Gudi Padwa Festival: As per Hindi Scriptures, it is believed that Lord Brahma created the universe on this day of Gudi Padwa and he was the one who introduced days, weeks, month and years to the world. As per other legends, it is also believed that it was the when when King Shalivahana celebrated the victory and people hoisted the flag when he came back to Paithan. Gudi is considered as a symbol of victory over evil. That's why people carried out the processions with a large number of people and carry a flag in their hands.

Fragrance of jasmine blooms a star attraction in Dagdusheth

The Mogra Festival at Shreemant Dagdusheth Halwai Ganpati Temple is a mesmerizing blend of spirituality and sensory delight. During this celebration, Bappa is immersed in Vasantik Utee (sandalwood paste, specially ordered from Southern parts of India). The intoxicating fragrance of mogra (jasmine) flowers fills the air as devotees gather to celebrate Lord Ganesha amidst this exquisite ambiance. The event features devotional performances, cultural displays, and bhajans, creating a unique fusion of reverence and festivity. The Mogra Festival captures the temple's dedication to engaging the senses and deepening the spiritual connection of its attendees.
